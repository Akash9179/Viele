## 1. Essence

**Viele helps you see fashion on people built like you — and feel like it was made for you.**

Most fashion media shows idealized, hard-to-relate-to bodies. Viele inverts that: open it and within about a minute you see real outfits worn by people with your shape, coloring, and aesthetic, each with a visible match. The brand feeling is **recognition and belonging**.

**The memorable thing:** *"made for a body like mine."*

---

## 2. Positioning

- **Category:** personalized fashion discovery.
- **For:** anyone who wants style inspiration that actually reflects their body — all shapes, sizes, genders, and complexions, day one.
- **Against:** the idealized, one-body-type fashion feed; the cold e-commerce catalog.
- **Wedge:** "outfits on people like you," made visible through the match score.

---

## 3. Personality

Confident, warm, quietly premium. A well-dressed friend with great taste who is never preachy or exclusionary. Editorial sensibility (like a good print fashion title) but **native and modern** in execution — it lives on your phone and feels like a first-class iOS citizen.

Five adjectives: **warm · confident · inclusive · refined · effortless.**

---

## 4. Voice & tone

- **Plain, warm, human.** Short sentences. Speak to one person.
- **Reassuring on anything sensitive** (body, coloring, weight): no judgment, always optional, explain the why.
- **Never hype, never clinical.** Not "optimize your profile" — rather "this helps us show you people built like you."
- **Confident, not loud.** Let the photography carry the emotion.

**Do / Don't**
- ✅ "Outfits on people built like you." ❌ "AI-powered personalized fashion recommendations."
- ✅ "No better or worse shape — this just helps us match you." ❌ "Select your body type."
- ✅ "Weight stays private — used only to improve matches." ❌ "Enter your weight."
- ✅ "There's genuinely no wrong answer." ❌ "Choose the correct skin tone."

---

## 5. Wordmark & logo

- **Now:** the wordmark "Viele" is set in **San Francisco** (SF Pro Display, heavy, tight tracking) — native, clean, consistent with the app. A lowercase/period flourish ("Viele.") is acceptable as a light signature.
- **Later:** a custom-drawn wordmark/logomark can be commissioned once the product is proven. Until then, do **not** use a decorative web serif as the logo (reads as a generic template).

---

## 6. Color meaning

- **Cream / warm neutrals** — calm, premium, lets clothing be the color story. The brand canvas.
- **Ink** — seriousness and clarity; the voice of the UI.
- **Match green** — the single signal of value: "this is for you." Used *only* for matching/positive, never decoration. It is the brand's one bright note.

(Exact values: `docs/design.md` §2.)

---

## 7. Photography & imagery direction

Imagery is the product. The brief for all in-app photography (and any marketing):

- **Real, body-diverse people** in real outfits — every shape, size, age, and complexion. This is the whole promise; casting is the brand.
- **Warm, natural light.** Soft, true-to-life color. Minimal heavy filtering or skin smoothing.
- **Relatable, not idealized.** Confident everyday people, not unattainable editorial fantasy. The anti-catalog.
- **Framing** that shows the silhouette: full-figure or 3/4.
- **Never** gradient/placeholder fills, sterile studio posing, or a single body type. Empty or fake imagery is off-brand.

---

## 8. Inclusivity ethos (load-bearing)

Because Viele asks people about their bodies and coloring, how we ask *is* the brand.

- Body shape and skin tone are **always optional and skippable**; matching still works without them.
- Use the **recognizable industry-standard shape names** (Hourglass, Pear, Rectangle, Apple/Round, Inverted Triangle) — people self-identify with them instantly (research, 2026-06-15) — **paired with a plain descriptor** and framed as "what feels closest." The kindness is in the **framing, not in avoiding the words**: optional, reassuring ("no better or worse"), and **non-prescriptive** — Viele *matches* you to similar people, it never tells you the "right/wrong" way to dress for your shape (which is the actual harm in the old "fruit body shape" advice).
- **Skin tone** uses the inclusive **Monk 10-tone** scale, matched on closeness ("no wrong answer"), never an exact judged value.
- **Weight is private** — owner-only, used only to improve matching as a coarse band, never shown to anyone.
- Be transparent: clearly say what's public (shape, height, coloring, aesthetic, shown with posts) vs private (weight), in plain words, layered through the flow.

This ethos is non-negotiable and overrides any UX shortcut that would make these inputs feel clinical, mandatory, or exposing.

---

## 9. Experience principles

- **Value before friction:** the wow (matched feed) comes before any account.
- **Native and fast:** it should feel like an Apple app — instant, familiar gestures, no web seams.
- **Quiet UI, loud content:** the interface gets out of the way of the photography.
- **Open and public (v1):** posts are public so everyone benefits from a full, diverse feed (private/followers come later).
